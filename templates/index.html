Federal Register Claims Search

Search 35,686 claims published in the Federal Register on March 31 and November 7, 1983, including forced fee patents, secretarial transfers, unapproved land sales, tax forfeitures, trespass, and other claim types.

These claims come from two 1983 Federal Register publications documenting injuries to Indian allottees and their heirs. Claim types include forced fee patents (land converted to fee status without consent), secretarial transfers, unapproved land sales, tax forfeitures, trespass, timber removal, and more. Use the Claim Type filter to explore specific categories.

Claims are organized by BIA agency code — an alphanumeric identifier from the Federal Register that encodes the BIA area office and local agency. The first letter indicates the area office (A = Aberdeen, B = Anadarko, C = Billings, F = Minneapolis, G = Muskogee, H = Phoenix, J = Sacramento, N = Navajo, P = Portland). Cancelled Patents

Some fee patents issued during the Dawes era — many of them forced fee patents issued without the allottee's application or consent — were later cancelled by federal action, restoring the land to restricted trust status. The principal pathways:

  • The 1927 Act (44 Stat. 1247) authorized the Secretary of the Interior to cancel fee patents issued without application or consent, on petition from the original allottee or heirs. The largest single category of cancellations.
  • The 1931 Act (46 Stat. 1471) extended cancellation authority to additional cases.
  • Acceptance refused — when an allottee refused to accept the fee patent on issuance, the patent could be cancelled administratively without legislation.
  • Individual orders, court decrees, and case-specific letters — including the May 22, 1916 "Caster Patent" letter (BIA letter #610929), the January 6, 1921 cancellation order, and individual court decrees.

A cancelled fee patent meant the land returned to restricted trust status. This was a partial, often delayed remedy for forced-fee dispossession — the cancellation typically came years or decades after the original conversion, and only for allottees (or heirs) who knew to petition.

This site documents 421 cancelled fee patents with the cancellation reason, dates, and BIA Central Classified File references compiled from primary BIA records — information not present in BLM's digital metadata. When a patent in this dataset appears on its patent detail page, the cancellation information is shown above the patent record. For an example, see Fred Laplant (1927 Act) or Abraham Generation (Caster Patent letter).
