How Indian Land Left Trust Status, 1948–57

During the termination decade, individual Indian land left trust status through many different legal mechanisms — sale, fee patent, public taking, removal of restrictions, and others. Murray Memorandum Table XII recorded the volume of these transactions by type, agency by agency. This page breaks that record down by mechanism.

Reading This Data

Agency-reported categories

The mechanism names are as each agency reported them in 1958. Agencies used different labels for the same act — one wrote "sales to fee status," another "sales to fee status or to non-Indians." These have been grouped into the families shown, but the grouping is an interpretive choice, not the document's own.

Reconciliation gap

The itemized mechanism counts do not always sum to an agency's reported gross total — the columns are not a mutually-exclusive partition, and {{ gap_count }} of {{ agencies_total }} agencies show a gap. Per-agency totals below report both figures so the discrepancy is visible.
