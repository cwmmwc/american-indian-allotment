Distribution of all 239,845 BLM allotment patents by year issued, showing fee patents vs. trust patents. The Murray Memorandum overlay (1948–1957) shows acres removed from trust during the termination era.

Policy Era Context

1887 — General Allotment Act (Dawes Act)
Authorized the President to survey and allot tribal lands to individual Indians, breaking up communal landholding.
1902 — Dead Indian Land Act
Allowed sale of inherited allotments, accelerating land loss.
1906 — Burke Act
Gave the Secretary of the Interior authority to issue fee patents to "competent" allottees before the end of the 25-year trust period, removing federal trust protections.
1907 — Declaration of Policy
Beginning of forced patenting under Commissioner Francis Leupp.
1917 — Sells Declaration / Competency Commissions
Commissioner Cato Sells declared all Indians of half or less Indian blood "competent," issuing fee patents without consent. Competency commissions traveled to reservations to assess allottees.
1920 — Order of Restoration
Secretary of the Interior John Barton Payne's order to stop forced patenting, though damage was largely done.
1934 — Indian Reorganization Act
Ended allotment and attempted to restore tribal self-governance.
1948–1957 — Termination Era (Murray Memorandum)
Congress pursued termination of federal trust responsibilities. The Murray Memorandum documents 2.6 million acres removed from individual Indian trust status during this period.
1953 — House Concurrent Resolution 108
Declared congressional policy to terminate federal supervision over selected tribes "at the earliest possible time."
