Reading This Data

Tables V and VII reconcile internally (the deduction categories sum to the deduction total; ownership categories sum to the 1934 total). The two tables measure different things and are not additive with each other: Table VII is the cumulative loss arc of tribal land up to 1934, while Table V is the composition of what remained in trust in 1934.

"Allotted" acreage (Table VII) is land carved out of tribal holdings and assigned to individuals; "alienated" is the portion of that already passed out of Indian ownership. Allotment is a separate channel from the ceded/surplus/miscellaneous tribal-land deductions, so the two should not be summed.

Source: Bureau of Indian Affairs land statistics compiled for the 1934 Wilson Report, Tables V (p. 5) and VII (p. 7), digitized at IATH. Derived tables: wilson_land_loss_by_state, wilson_ownership_1934_by_state.
